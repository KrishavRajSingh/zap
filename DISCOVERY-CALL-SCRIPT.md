# Discovery Call Script

Use this file during calls.

Your goal is not to sound impressive.

Your goal is:

- understand their workflow
- find the painful repetitive part
- see if Zap fits
- ask for a live walkthrough or supervised pilot

Do not try to sound like a polished salesperson.
Sound clear, calm, and curious.

## Read This Verbatim

If you are nervous, just read this word for word.

```text
Thanks for taking the time. I’ll keep this simple.

I’m building a browser-based assistant for repetitive portal work, especially where someone has to log into a website, fill forms, upload documents, check missing fields, and still stay in control before anything sensitive gets submitted.

I’m early, so I’m not trying to hard-sell anything. I’m mainly trying to understand how your team does this work today, where the pain is, and whether what I’m building is actually useful.

The most helpful thing for me would be if you could walk me through one real workflow from start to finish.

What is the most repetitive portal or browser-based work your team does every week?

Can you walk me through the last time someone did that workflow from start to finish?

Where do people re-enter the same information or upload the same documents again and again?

What part is the slowest or causes the most mistakes?

What part would you never want automated without approval?

What I’m hearing is that the painful part is [repeat their words simply]. That sounds like a strong fit for what I’m building.

Would you be open to a supervised test where you log in yourself, stay in control, and we try this in fill-but-don’t-submit mode?
```

## The Main Rule

You are not selling software on the first call.

You are saying:

- I am learning this workflow
- I built something related
- I want to understand how you do it today
- if it fits, I would love to test it with you safely

## 15-Minute Call Structure

Use this exact structure.

1. 1 minute: thank them and set context
2. 2 minutes: explain what you are building in plain English
3. 8 minutes: ask about their workflow
4. 3 minutes: reflect back what you heard and see if there is a fit
5. 1 minute: ask for next step

## Before the Call

Have these things open:

- the company website
- the contact's LinkedIn or profile
- a blank notes doc
- one short Zap demo if needed

Write these 4 things before joining:

- what kind of company this is
- why you think they fit
- what title the person has
- what next step you want if the call goes well

## Your Opener

Say this almost exactly.

```text
Thanks for taking the time. I’ll keep this simple.

I’m building a browser-based assistant for repetitive portal work, especially workflows where someone has to log into a website, fill forms, upload documents, check missing fields, and still stay in control before anything sensitive gets submitted.

I’m early, so I’m not trying to force a sale here. I mainly want to understand how your team does this kind of work today, where the pain is, and whether what I’m building is even useful.
```

Then say:

```text
The most helpful thing for me would be if you could walk me through one real workflow from start to finish.
```

## If They Say “Sure”

Go straight into this.

```text
Perfect. Could you walk me through the last time your team handled vendor or supplier portal work like onboarding, profile updates, document uploads, or compliance checks?
```

### Question 3

## Best Question On The Whole Call

If you remember only one thing, ask this:

```text
Can you show me the last time this was annoying?
```

This gets much better answers than:

- would you use AI for this?
- do you like automation?
- would this be useful?

## If They Start Talking Too Broadly

Bring them back with this:

```text
Could we make it concrete and use the last real example instead of the general process?
```

Then ask:

```text
What website did they log into?
What documents did they need?
What fields had to be filled?
Where did they get stuck?
```

## If You Need To Explain Zap Simply

Use this, not a complicated product pitch.

```text
The simple version is: Zap works inside the user’s real browser and helps with repetitive website tasks like filling forms, moving through portal steps, and preparing work faster.

The important part is that it can pause before risky actions, so the human stays in control.
```

## If They Ask “So What Exactly Does It Do?”

Say:

```text
The best current use case is repetitive portal work.

For example, if someone has to open a vendor portal, fill company profile details, upload documents, check missing fields, and stop before final submit, Zap can help with that in the browser they already use.
```

## If They Ask “Is It Fully Autonomous?”

Say:

```text
Not by default, and I think that’s the right approach for this kind of workflow.

Right now I’m focused on making it useful and safe first, especially where a human should approve anything sensitive.
```

## If They Ask “How Is This Different From Generic AI Tools?”

Say:

```text
Generic AI tools are good at answering questions.

What I’m focused on is actual in-browser work inside the user’s real session, with approval before risky actions and visibility into what happened.
```

## If They Ask “How Early Is This?”

Say:

```text
It’s early, but real. I already have the browser workflow running. Right now I’m narrowing it around one painful use case instead of pretending it solves everything.
```

## If They Ask “Who Is This For?”

Say:

```text
The best fit is teams that spend real time in portals doing repetitive browser work, especially where documents, form filling, and approval steps are involved.
```

## If They Ask About Security

Do not overtalk.

Say:

```text
The model I’m working toward is that the user stays in their real browser session, logs in themselves, and stays in control of sensitive actions.

For early pilots, I prefer supervised runs and approval before anything risky.
```

## If They Ask About Pricing Too Early

Say:

```text
I’m still in the learning and pilot stage, so the first goal is making sure this solves a real workflow well.

If it looks like a strong fit, I’d suggest a small fixed-scope pilot rather than trying to force a full pricing discussion too early.
```

## How To Transition From Questions To Next Step

Once they describe pain clearly, say this:

```text
What I’m hearing is that the painful part is [repeat their workflow in simple words].

That sounds like a strong fit for what I’m building, especially because [repeat their pain: repeated entry / uploads / portal checks / approval].
```

Then ask:

```text
Would you be open to a supervised test where you log in yourself, stay in control, and we try this in fill-but-don’t-submit mode?
```

## If They Are Interested But Busy

Say:

```text
No problem. The easiest next step would be either:

1. a 20-minute workflow walkthrough with the person who does this work most often, or
2. a short supervised test on one real example.
```

## If They Say “This Sounds Interesting”

Do not stop there.

Say:

```text
Great. Interesting is a good start, but what matters to me is whether this is a real pain.

How often does this happen, and how much time does it actually take today?
```

## If They Say “We Already Have Tools”

Say:

```text
That makes sense. I’m not assuming your current setup is broken.

I’m mainly curious whether there is still a repetitive browser step where people are doing manual portal work, document uploads, or repeated checks that existing tools don’t really remove.
```

## If They Say “Not My Area”

Say:

```text
Totally fair. Is there someone in operations, procurement, AP, vendor onboarding, or compliance who owns that workflow?
```

## Good Endings

Use one of these.

### Ending A: Ask For Walkthrough

```text
This was helpful. The best next step would be a live walkthrough of one real example. Would you be open to that?
```

### Ending B: Ask For Pilot

```text
This feels like a real fit. Would you be open to a supervised pilot on one workflow, with you staying in control and no final submit by default?
```

### Ending C: Ask For Referral

```text
This was useful. If you’re not the right owner, who on your team is closest to this workflow day to day?
```

## What Good Signal Looks Like

Good signals:

- they can name the exact portal
- they can name the exact documents
- they say this happens often
- they complain about repetitive entry or uploads
- they care about approval and mistakes
- they agree to a walkthrough or pilot

Bad signals:

- they stay vague
- they say it rarely happens
- they think it is cool but not urgent
- they cannot identify an owner
- they do not want to spend more time on it

## What Not To Do On The Call

- do not over-explain AI
- do not promise full autonomy
- do not give a giant product demo too early
- do not argue with their workflow
- do not talk more than they do
- do not leave without asking for a next step

## Your Only Job

At the end of the call, you want one of these outcomes.

1. a live workflow walkthrough
2. a supervised pilot
3. a referral to the right person

If you get one of those, the call was useful.
